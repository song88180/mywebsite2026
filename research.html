Research

Zhang Lab, Department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology, University of Michigan

09/05/2019 - present

During my PhD studies, I explored selection in molecular and phenotypic evolution using large genotype and phenotype datasets. Projects include a quasi-neutral model of molecular evolution, genetic studies of human same-sex sexual behavior, and analyses of human sex ratio at birth.

Lepidoptera LW opsin evolution in diurnal and nocturnal species

Pierce Lab, Harvard University | 10/06/2017 - 04/01/2018

I studied the evolution of opsin genes to compare spectral sensitivities between diurnal and nocturnal Lepidoptera species. The project included screening long wavelength opsins, building expression constructs, and mining insect genome databases for opsin motifs.

Phylogeny of Ambystomatidae

Shaffer Lab, UCLA | 07/09/2017 - 09/15/2017

This project used targeted gene enrichment to reveal the phylogeny of Ambystomatidae. I selected representative samples, performed DNA extraction and library preparation, and learned core workflows for processing NGS data and SNP calling.

Hymenoptera taxonomy and phylogeny practice

Xuexin Chen Lab, Zhejiang University | 11/01/2015 - 07/2017

I studied Hymenoptera morphology, taxonomy, and phylogenetic analysis. This included microscope-based specimen classification, family-level keys, and mitochondrial DNA evolution analyses using tools such as MrBayes, RAxML, PAML, and BEAST.
